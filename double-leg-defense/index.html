Double Leg Defense

Double leg defense is the set of actions used to stop a partner from connecting to both legs and driving through to finish a takedown.

Foundational concept Movements Common Sprawl Defense Level Change Shot Entry

Quick definition

Double leg defense is the set of actions used to stop a partner from connecting to both legs and driving through to finish a takedown.

Beginner translation

Double leg defense means shutting down the shot before the attacker can connect, lift, or run through the hips.

Why it matters

Double leg defense is the set of actions used to stop a partner from connecting to both legs and driving through to finish a takedown.

What beginners should know

A common mistake is sprawling straight back without addressing the upper body or angle, which can still leave the defense unstable.

Common class phrases

  • Double leg defense connects to sprawl defense, level change, shot entry, head position, and double leg.
  • Double Leg Defense in drills
  • Double Leg Defense during positional rounds

Beginner Safety Cue

Safety cue: Move with control and communicate early while training double leg defense.

FAQ

Is double leg defense just sprawling?

No. Effective defense also needs head, hands, hip pressure, and angle.

What helps most after stuffing a shot?

Re-establish posture and angle before countering or resetting.
