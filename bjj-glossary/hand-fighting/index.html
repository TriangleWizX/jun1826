Hand Fighting

Hand fighting is the standing battle for grip, tie, and inside control that sets up safer entries and better positioning.

Foundational concept Basics Common Inside Position Collar Tie Pummel

Quick definition

Hand fighting is the standing battle for grip, tie, and inside control that sets up safer entries and better positioning.

Beginner translation

Hand fighting is the arm-and-hand battle that happens before bigger standing attacks.

Why it matters

Hand fighting is the standing battle for grip, tie, and inside control that sets up safer entries and better positioning.

What beginners should know

A common mistake with hand fighting is using it passively instead of with structure and timing.

Common class phrases

  • Hand fighting connects to inside position
  • collar tie
  • pummel
  • arm drag
  • and takedown

Beginner Safety Cue

Safety cue: Move with control and communicate early while training hand fighting.

Inside Position

Inside position means winning the space between your partner's arms or legs so your connections are more direct, safer, and easier to build from.

Read more →

Collar Tie

A collar tie is a standing control where one hand rests behind or near the back of the partner's head or neck to manage posture, direction, and distance.

Read more →

Pummel

To pummel means to swim your arms inside and around your partner's arms to fight for better upper-body position and control.

Read more →

Arm Drag

An arm drag is a movement that guides your partner's arm across your center line to create an angle and expose the side or back.

Read more →

Takedown

A takedown is a movement or sequence that brings the fight from standing to the ground while aiming to land in a better position.

FAQ

Is hand fighting only for competition?

No. It is a core standing skill for control and safety in normal training.

What improves hand fighting fastest?

Posture, footwork, and consistent pummeling/entry drills.
