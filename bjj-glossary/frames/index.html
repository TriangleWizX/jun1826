What frames mean in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu

Frames are body structures, usually made with the forearms, hands, knees, or shins, that create space and keep pressure from collapsing onto you. A frame is one of those structures used well.

Quick definition

Frames are body structures you build to stop pressure from collapsing onto you and to make space. A frame is one of those support posts built with your body.

Beginner translationFrames are the walls you build with your body so the other person cannot smash all the way into you.
Why it mattersGood frames turn panic into structure and make escapes and recoveries understandable.
Common next termsShrimp, Guard, Side Control, Knee Shield

Frames in plain English

Frames are one of the first defensive ideas that make BJJ feel manageable. Instead of trying to press someone off you with strength, you build smart structures with your arms, knees, or shins so their weight has somewhere to stop before it crushes you. When someone says build a frame, they usually mean create one of those strong posts with your body.

Frames do not win with force. They win by creating shape and space.

What they do

Frames help protect your neck, hips, and chest while giving you space to recover guard, shrimp, or stand up. One good frame can completely change a bad position by slowing the pressure down enough for your next move to become possible.

Where you see them

You see frames during side control escapes, mount defense, guard recovery, knee-shield structure, and many standing exchanges. Coaches teach them early because they show up almost everywhere a beginner feels stuck.

Why they matter

Frames matter because they are one of the first real defensive skills that make beginners feel calmer. Once students understand what a single frame does, the larger idea of frames as a system starts making much more sense.
