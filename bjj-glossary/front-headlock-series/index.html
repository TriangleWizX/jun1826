What front headlock series means in Brazilian Jiu Jitsu

A front headlock series is a connected group of controls, transitions, and attacks that begin from front head control and branch into follow-up options.

Beginner translation

Instead of one move, it is the whole chain that comes after you control the head from the front.

What it does

The front headlock series connects snap downs, go-behinds, spin-behinds, chokes, and positional upgrades into one system instead of isolated moves.

Where you see it

You see front headlock series work after sprawls, failed shots, turtle reactions, and standing hand-fighting exchanges that break posture forward.

Why it matters

Beginners learn that strong grapplers do not hit one move and stop. They connect one control point to the next likely answer.

Related terms

  • Snap Down
  • Spin Behind
  • Go Behind
