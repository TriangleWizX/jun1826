Chest to Back

Chest to back means staying tightly connected to your partner's back with your chest so they have less room to turn, shake you off, or escape.

Foundational concept Positions Common Back Control Seatbelt Hooks

Quick definition

Chest to back means staying tightly connected to your partner's back with your chest so they have less room to turn, shake you off, or escape.

Beginner translation

Chest to back is how you stay glued to someone when you are behind them.

Why it matters

Chest to back means staying tightly connected to your partner's back with your chest so they have less room to turn, shake you off, or escape.

What beginners should know

A common mistake with chest to back is using it passively instead of with structure and timing.

Common class phrases

  • Chest to back connects to back control
  • seatbelt
  • hooks
  • back take
  • and rear naked choke

Beginner Safety Cue

Safety cue: Move with control and communicate early while training chest to back.

Seatbelt

The seatbelt is a back-control grip where one arm goes over the shoulder and the other arm goes under the armpit to connect around the torso.

Hooks

Hooks are foot or leg connections used to control, lift, steer, or stay attached to a partner. The term is most often used for back control hooks and butterfly hooks.

Read more →

Back Take

A back take is the act of moving behind your partner and establishing strong back control before they can turn back to face you.

Read more →

Rear Naked Choke

A rear naked choke is a choke applied from the back where the attacker uses the arms to compress the neck and force a safe tap.

FAQ

Is chest-to-back control only about hooks?

No. Hooks help, but chest connection is a major control factor.

Why do people lose back control quickly?

They create space by leaning away instead of staying connected.
