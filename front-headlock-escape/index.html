What front headlock escape means in Brazilian Jiu Jitsu

A front headlock escape is the process of clearing head control, rebuilding posture, and getting back to a safer position before the attacker can settle stronger control or attack.

Beginner translation

You free your head and neck from the front so the other person cannot keep folding you down.

What it does

Front headlock escapes stop snap-down follow-ups, reduce choke danger, and help you recover posture, base, or a better angle.

Where you see it

You see front headlock escapes after failed shots, sprawls, turtle situations, and standing exchanges where the head drops too low.

Why it matters

Beginners learn that once the head gets controlled, the rest of the body often follows. Escaping early matters.
