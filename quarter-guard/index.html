Quarter Guard

Quarter guard is a control point where the bottom player traps only part of one leg instead of controlling the leg as fully as in half guard.

Foundational concept Positions Common Half Guard Guard Pass Knee Cut

Quick definition

Quarter guard is a control point where the bottom player traps only part of one leg instead of controlling the leg as fully as in half guard.

Beginner translation

Quarter guard is a thinner, weaker version of half guard. You still have some connection to the leg, but not enough to control it the same way.

Why it matters

Quarter guard is a control point where the bottom player traps only part of one leg instead of controlling the leg as fully as in half guard.

What beginners should know

A common mistake with quarter guard is using it passively instead of with structure and timing.

Beginner Safety Cue

Safety cue: Move with control and communicate early while training quarter guard.

Half Guard

Guard Pass

A guard pass is the act of getting around, through, or past the bottom player's legs so the top player can reach stronger control like side control, north south, or mount.

Knee Cut

A knee cut is a guard pass where the top player slices the knee across the bottom player's legs to move toward side control or another strong top position.

Read more →

Guard Retention

Guard retention is the skill of keeping your guard or rebuilding it when the top player is trying to pass around, through, or over your legs.

Top Position

Top position means you are above your partner and usually have a better chance to apply pressure, pass, and control movement.
