What happens

Your first class is not a test.

Sensei Sandy teaches beginners in a small Tannersville room with clear steps, careful partner matching, and enough explanation that you know what is happening next.

1

Meet the room

You see where class starts, where to put your things, and how the studio works before training begins.

2

Learn the safety rules

You learn how to tap, how partners care for each other, and what controlled training should feel like.

3

Start with skill

You warm up, learn fundamentals, and work at a beginner pace before intensity ever becomes the focus.

Why this feels different

Calm beginner-friendly martial arts means more than a soft slogan.

It means the first visit is organized, grappling is taught without striking, questions are expected, and the coach gives you one clear next step instead of leaving you to decode the room alone.

If you are an adult beginner in the Catskills, start with the adult program. If you are choosing for a child, use the kids program. If you want one-on-one support first, see private lessons.

Questions

First-Timer FAQ

How is the first class paced for first-timers?

The first visit is built around a room tour, safety walkthrough, Beginner Lane orientation, and a clear next step.

What happens in my first Jiu-Jitsu class?

You meet Sandy, see the room, learn how tapping works, get paired carefully, and move through beginner-paced instruction before any intensity increases.

Do I have to spar hard on day one?

No. The first class is skill-first and coached. Beginners start with safety, movement, and controlled partner work.

What if I want a quieter first step?

Private lessons are available for beginners who want one-on-one coaching before or alongside group classes.
