Escape

An escape is the act of moving from a bad or restricted position to a safer, stronger, or more neutral one.

Foundational concept Movements Common Frame Hip Escape Elbow Knee Escape

Quick definition

An escape is the act of moving from a bad or restricted position to a safer, stronger, or more neutral one.

Beginner translation

An escape is how you stop being stuck.

Why it matters

An escape is the act of moving from a bad or restricted position to a safer, stronger, or more neutral one.

What beginners should know

A common mistake with escape is using it passively instead of with structure and timing.

Common class phrases

Beginner Safety Cue

Safety cue: Move with control and communicate early while training escape.

Hip Escape

A hip escape is a movement where you shift your hips away to create space and improve your position. It is the formal name for a shrimp movement.

Elbow Knee Escape

An elbow knee escape is a mount escape where the bottom player uses the elbow and knee together to create space and recover guard or half guard.

Read more →

Upa Escape

An upa escape is a mount escape where the bottom player traps part of the top player's base and bridges to reverse the position.
