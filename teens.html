More Than Just a Workout

Middle school is loud. We give them a quiet place to build strength.

If your teen slouches through the day, lives in their hoodie, or disappears into a phone when things get awkward, you’re not alone.

This is the "On-Ramp" to real confidence.

The Teens 6-Week Resilience Challenge is the dedicated teen version of our Grand Slam program. It is designed to get beginners ready.

This challenge gives them something solid to stand on: a structured place to push themselves, build strength, and practice staying calm when things get stressful—without you having to nag.

👉Try the Daily Dozen Mobility Routine

Who is this for?

  • The Shy Teen: Disappears into their phone when life gets loud.
  • The Intense Teen: Has a short fuse and needs to channel energy.
  • The Athlete: Plays other sports but battles nerves.
  • The Seeker: Needs solid role models outside of school.

What The Challenge Builds

Real Confidence

Not “I’m fine” on repeat, but the kind that comes from getting taken down, getting back up, and seeing they can handle it.

Emotional Control

Breathing, posture, and decision-making when someone is grabbing, resisting, or trying to pin them.

Strong Posture

Core strength, grip, balance, and movement that carry over into other sports (and how they walk down the hallway).

Communication

Learning to stand tall, hold eye contact, and use their voice clearly in stressful situations.

Respect & Teamwork

Zero "tough guy" nonsense. Winning and losing with respect, and taking care of training partners.

"It's a third place—not home, not school—where they can just focus and grow."

Safety & Standards

  • Supervised Contact Only: Clear rules, consistent routines, no chaos.
  • "Tap Early" Culture: We prioritize safety over ego.
  • Phones Away: A true digital detox for the hour they are with us.
  • Clean Hygienic Mats: We maintain the highest standards of cleanliness.

How The First Day Works

Reserve Spot

Claim a place in the Teens Challenge so we can plan partners and welcome your teen by name.

Arrive 10 Mins Early

Comfortable clothes, water bottle, bare feet. We’ll introduce the coach and show them where to line up.

Learn Basics

They’ll learn how to bow in, stay safe, and exactly what to do if they feel overwhelmed. No guessing.
