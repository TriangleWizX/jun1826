What sweep single leg takedown means in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu

A sweep single leg takedown is a wrestling-style single-leg entry and finish where you collect one leg, off-balance the person, and bring them down under control.

Quick definition

The sweep single is a single leg takedown that uses angle, balance, and control of one leg to finish the entry cleanly.

Beginner translationYou catch one leg, move them out of balance, and bring them down before they can recover.
Why it mattersThis teaches beginners that takedowns are about angle and control, not just blasting forward.

Sweep single leg takedown in plain English

The sweep single is a takedown idea that helps beginners understand that standing exchanges still follow the same BJJ logic: off-balance first, control the angle, and finish with structure instead of rushing. Even though the term comes from wrestling language, the underlying lesson fits BJJ very well.

A good sweep single wins the angle before it tries to win the takedown.

What it does

The sweep single helps a student collect one leg, shift the opponent’s balance, and convert a standing exchange into a takedown or dominant top follow-up. It also teaches how to keep moving once the leg is captured instead of stalling in a weak position.

Where you see it

You see sweep singles in standing grappling, wrestling-style entries, takedown drills, and scrambles where one leg becomes exposed. It can also show up as a follow-up when a deeper shot does not finish the first time.

Why it matters

The sweep single matters because it gives beginners a concrete example of takedown mechanics without needing to treat every entry like a collision. It teaches angle, control of the leg, and how movement creates the finish.
