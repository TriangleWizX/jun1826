Back Defense

Back defense is the set of habits and movements used to protect the neck, disrupt control, and recover safer positions when an opponent is behind you.

Foundational concept Movements Common Seatbelt Back Control Rear Naked Choke

Quick definition

Back defense is the set of habits and movements used to protect the neck, disrupt control, and recover safer positions when an opponent is behind you.

Beginner translation

Back defense means staying safe when someone is on your back and trying to attack your neck.

Why it matters

What beginners should know

A common mistake with back defense is using it passively instead of with structure and timing.

The seatbelt is a back-control grip where one arm goes over the shoulder and the other arm goes under the armpit to connect around the torso.

FAQ

What is first in back defense?

Protect the neck and hand-fight the choking arm before explosive movement.

Can back defense lead to escape?

Yes. Good defense creates the conditions for safer escapes and recovery.
