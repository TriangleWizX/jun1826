### Option B  -  The Logic (Safety, Structure, Science)

If you’re curious about BJJ but worried about getting hurt, read this:

Good training isn’t chaos. It’s a system.

In a beginner-friendly class, you can expect:

- Clean mats and simple hygiene rules (because safety starts there)
- A scalable warm-up (movement practice, not punishment)
- Technique taught as cooperative learning (slow, coached, repeatable)
- Clear boundaries and permission to opt out of anything on Day 1

And the biggest one:

**Beginners do not spar on Day 1.**

I broke the whole first class down minute-by-minute so you know what you’re walking into before you ever step on the mat.
